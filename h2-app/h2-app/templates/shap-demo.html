SHAP

SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) values are used for explaining the output of a model. They assign a score to each feature indicating the degree to which it contributed towards a prediction. Negative shap scores lead to a decrease in the prediction, while positive shap scores lead to an increase. .

The most basic one is demographic parity. It means that the model score should be independent of the protected attribute which in this case is race. This would imply that the shap score for race should be zero.

Equalized Odds

Another discrimination criterion is equalized odds, which means that the score should be independent of the protected attributed when conditioned on the target result.

Calibrated Equalized Odds

There is also another method which achieves calibrated equalized odds using derived predictors. However, instead of flipping predictions it changes them to the mean of all the predictions. The details are somewhat involved, but similar to the original method, the instances to change are randomly determined. SHAP is used to select the most disadvantaged and the most advantaged people and change their predictions, again resulting in similar group fairness but better individual fairness. Accuracy drops, but the derived predictor is more fair. This is expected due to the fairness-accuracy tradeoff.
